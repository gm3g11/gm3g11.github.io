About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Notre Dame, working under the supervision of Prof. Erin W. Chambers and Prof. Danny Z. Chen. My research develops efficient and interpretable methods at the intersection of machine learning, computational geometry, and topological data analysis.

My current focus is on medical image analysis by integrating deep learning with persistent homology for self-supervised learning and clustering. I have developed frameworks that achieve state-of-the-art performance on multiple medical imaging benchmarks, including VTRL-Med (81.51% accuracy across 13 datasets) and TopoCL (+3.26% improvement with statistical significance across five contrastive learning methods).

I also work on computational topology, optimal transport algorithms, and curriculum learning strategies for efficient large language model fine-tuning. My work on NNS-EMD achieved 44× to 135× speedup over exact Earth Mover's Distance computation, while my PUDF framework demonstrated 75.48% faster training compared to state-of-the-art methods.

Research Interests
