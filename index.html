About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Notre Dame, working under the supervision of Prof. Erin W. Chambers and Prof. Danny Z. Chen. My research develops efficient and interpretable methods at the intersection of machine learning, computational geometry, and topological data analysis.

My work bridges classical computational geometry with modern deep learning, applying topological methods to enhance understanding and performance of machine learning systems. I am particularly interested in leveraging persistent homology and optimal transport to enable more robust and interpretable representations in medical imaging and other application domains.

Research Interests

  • Medical Image Analysis with Topological Data Analysis
  • Self-Supervised Learning and Contrastive Learning
  • Computational Geometry and Optimal Transport
  • Curriculum Learning for Large Language Models
  • Persistent Homology and Shape Analysis
